Mapping Nietzsche

In Beyond Good and Evil, Friedrich Nietzsche includes a list of one hundred and twenty-three "Epigrams and Interludes." This graph maps them onto a two-dimensonal space. The coordinates are arbitrary, but distance reflects similarity and color clustering. Hover over the points to display the text of the aphorism, or search by keyword or number.

How to Build It

I started by creating a list of aphorisms in the original German. Then, I parsed them to create a word-frequency matrix (each row is an aphorism, each column a word, and each cell the number of times the word appears in the aphorism). I calculated the tf-idf (term frequencyâ€“inverse document frequency), which measures the frequency of the word in each aphorism inversely weighted by how many times the word appears across all of the aphorisms. Finally, I applied t-SNE (t-distributed stochastic neighbors embedding) for dimensonality reduction and generated hiearchical clusters.
